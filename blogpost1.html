5 Things to Look for in a Child Transportation Service

Published on June 8, 2025 | By The Nanny Driver Team

Entrusting someone with your child's transportation is one of the most significant decisions a parent can make. In a world of countless options, how do you sift through the noise to find a service that offers genuine peace of mind? It comes down to looking for the right signals of safety, professionalism, and care.

Here are five non-negotiable things every parent should look for in a child transportation service.

1. Rigorous, Multi-Layered Driver Vetting

This is the most critical factor. "Background checked" can mean many things, so you need to know what's really being checked. A trustworthy service will be transparent about its process.

  • Comprehensive Criminal Checks: Go beyond a simple database search. Look for services that perform national, state, and local county-level checks.
  • Continuous DMV Monitoring: A clean driving record at the time of hiring isn't enough. The best services continuously monitor their drivers' records for any new infractions.
  • Child-Specific Clearances: Ensure the service checks against child abuse and sex offender registries.

2. Employee Drivers, Not Independent Contractors

The distinction between a W-2 employee and a 1099 "gig worker" is massive when it comes to accountability and training. Employee drivers are managed, trained, and held to a consistent company standard. They are part of a team dedicated to a mission, not just a person picking up a ride for a quick fare. This model ensures a higher level of reliability and professionalism.

3. Transparent Technology with Parental Controls

Technology should empower you, not leave you with more questions. A modern, safe service should offer:

  • Real-Time GPS Tracking: You should be able to see your child's journey from start to finish on a map within an app.
  • Live Notifications: Receive automatic alerts for key moments, such as "Driver Arrived," "Child Picked Up," and "Child Safely Dropped Off."
  • Secure Communication Channels: The ability to contact the driver or a support team directly through the app without sharing personal phone numbers.

4. A Strong, Public Commitment to Safety Protocols

Safety isn't just a feature; it's a culture. Look for a company that proudly and publicly details its safety standards. This includes driver training in defensive driving and child-specific situations, clear emergency response protocols, and policies regarding in-vehicle monitoring like dashcams.

5. Excellent, Accessible Customer Support

When you have a question or concern about your child, you need to be able to reach a real person quickly. Look for services that offer dedicated support channels (phone, in-app chat) staffed by a team that understands the unique needs of parents.
